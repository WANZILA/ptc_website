Dear Friends! Warm Greetings in the Name of our Lord Jesus Christ! It is with immense joy and delight that,I learn of your and keenness to learn more about the programmes offered at Pentecostal Theological College,

Mbale- Uganda. Welcome! Our Bible College has been established to equip and send forth well-trained and Spirit-filled Christian servant leaders who will work towards fulfilling the Great Commission of Jesus Christ (Matt. 28: 18-20). Our educational mandate is clearly seen in the obligation to carry out the teaching and equipping task for the church as enshrined in the Great Commission mandate. The Bible College is committed to offer scholastic training for the varied church and community leaders, with the carefully designed curriculum emphasizing; Bible based teaching that is Evangelical in Theology and Community Transformation. Hence, our College offers Diploma in Bible and Theology and Certificate in Church Ministries. PTC is a family where each person lives for the other, and all live for God. The presence of the faculty and students from various denominational, cultural, ethnic, and linguistic backgrounds enrich the community life of the college. The prayer groups, talent shows in sports, etc. helps the members to celebrate the oneness in the other. The faculty supervised fieldwork exposure provides opportunity for our students to translate the academic learning in class to practical field ministry experience. The farm in the college also provides the comprehensive approach and environment to the training that is the unique to our wholistic goal in leadership training; especially for our student pastors. Looking forward to welcoming you to the Pentecostal Theological College, Mbale. In His Mission we Go! .

I am happy you have chosen to become a member of the Pentecostal Theological College Family! The Pentecostal Theological College and the Office of the Dean of Students are your partners in your quest to reach your academic and personal goals. Orientation is the first step on this remarkable journey. During orientation, you will have the opportunity to learn more about strategies for adjusting to college successfully,

getting involved on campus, and making good choices. These sessions provide practical and valuable information on our policies and the many programs and services we have in place to promote a healthy environment, supportive of student success. I encourage you to attend these sessions. The set of expectations for PTC students provides a clear path to student success at PTC, encouraging students to EXCEL, STRETCH, ENGAGE, CHOOSE, and SERVE. The expectations encourage students to choose wisely and make decisions of which they, their families, and their institution can be proud. I am confident in your ability to meet these expectations and we will do all we can to support you in doing so. We hope you will embrace these PTC expectations and
  1. EXCEL academically
  2. STRETCH yourself and try something new
  3. ENGAGE on campus and in the community
  4. CHOOSE to make decisions that will elevate you and your future
  5. SERVE God, the campus and community

As you choose your path, we dont want high-risk behaviors to spoil your orientation experience or to jeopardize the considerable investment you are about to make in your education. We are committed to helping all students be successful at PTC. During orientation, you will learn about bystander intervention, which is one of the ways we are working to ensure safety on campus. We all have a role in campus safety - we are counting on you to play an active part. There are College Regulations and Rules that all students must abide by. The College Regulations cover programs and examinations, library, registration among others, and there are College rules on the students conduct obtained from the Dean of Students Office in the ADMIN 1 building.

The College Affairs and Disciplinary Committee deals with disciplinary matters of students. It is advisable that students avoid acts which might make them appear before the College Disciplinary Committee. The College is non-tolerant to acts of hooliganism and all sorts of immorality. students should ensure that they read all the college regulations and rules, and abide by them.

A high degree of spiritual excellence is sought and encouraged by both faculty and students body. Private devotion, morning and evening, along with daily chapel services in which students and faculty participates, helps to achieve this goal: In addition, College is an amazing time of life - I hope you will make the most of it!
