Our library is charged with the responsibility of serving the reading, reference and research needs of students, and staff of the College of Pentecostals. Therefore, Pentecostal theological library is built with a collection containing the materials that best suit the objectives of that clientele both now and in the future...

It supports the educational, teaching, research, and administrative needs of the whole college, students, and staff of Pentecostal theological college. The library takes an active role in supporting the overall mission of PTC by providing access to relevant information through the development of library collections, their organization and arrangement for access and use, and the provision of appropriate information and instructional services. To be most effective in fulfilling its mission, the library offers an array of basic services funded by the College for current students, faculty, and staff; other services to this primary clientele may be offered on a full or partial cost recovery basis.

The library is committed to making it's unique or distinctive collections available to the local, regional, and national scholarly community as a supplementary source once local resources have been exhausted. The library provides access for these users through either onsite consultation or other methods under conditions that do not jeopardize the effectiveness of it's services to it's primary clientele. In addition, the library makes it's services and collections available to the public at large on a limited basis to

  • Church pastors Library cardholders
  • Church bishops Library cardholders
  • Neighbor theological Colleges of Mbale cardholders
  • Alumni cardholders
  • Non teaching staff card holders
  • Students, faculty, and staff
  • Immediate family of PTC faculty and staff
  • Retired PTC faculty (residing near the college)

Welcome to Pentecostal Theological College Library

A library with a relevant and adequate collection is not built in a single month or year. It's a gradual process that takes time and with a carefully thought collection of development policy. With this in mind, we at Pentecostal Theological College celebrate our strengths and pride while recognizing what remains to be done to realize our vision.

The College Already has a solid foundation of print textbooks and some electronic open access databases to fill the information needs of our students, faculty staff and other scholars. Our Library is a learning center on campus, an environment where students can read from, use computers or textbooks, study and innovate. It is a place for our students to enrich their academic life and be inspired to set and reach higher goals as we strive to produce ethical and responsible Christian leaders.

The success of our Library so far has depended on our continuity to align library goals with the goals of the college as a whole. Therefore, the library department continues to provide leadership in the planning and development of Academic programmes that are central in strengthening and enhancing the vision of the college. We, at Pentecostal Theological College Library strive to serve our Students even better.
